# Dimension 23.01: Autonomy Boundary

## Purpose

Study what the system believes agents should do autonomously vs with human review.

## Steps

1. Identify where autonomy boundaries are defined.
2. Check which actions are always autonomous vs always gated.
3. Inspect whether autonomy level is configurable.
4. Check whether autonomy decisions are documented.
5. Check whether the system escalates appropriately.

## Evidence to Capture

- Autonomy configs
- Autonomy level definitions
- Gating policies
- Autonomy documentation

## Questions

1. What determines agent autonomy?
2. Are autonomy levels configurable?
3. Are boundaries documented?
4. Does the system respect autonomy boundaries?

## Rating

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Absent, implicit, ad-hoc, or unsafe |
| 4-6 | Present but inconsistent, weakly documented, or fragile |
| 7-8 | Clear model with tests, explicit interfaces, and operational safeguards |
| 9-10 | Mature, durable, observable, extensible, and proven under failure or scale |

> Does the system know when it is out of its depth?

# Dimension 13.04: Recovery vs Escalation

## Purpose

Study when the system keeps trying, asks the user, escalates to a human, or stops.

## Steps

1. Identify recovery paths for common failures.
2. Check which failures trigger automatic retry vs human escalation.
3. Inspect escalation policies.
4. Check whether escalation decisions are configurable.
5. Check whether failed recovery attempts are logged for audit.

## Evidence to Capture

- Recovery handlers
- Escalation triggers
- Human notification code
- Escalation policy config
- Recovery audit logs

## Questions

1. When does the system retry vs escalate?
2. Are escalation thresholds configurable?
3. Can the system stop gracefully?
4. Are recovery decisions auditable?

## Rating

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Absent, implicit, ad-hoc, or unsafe |
| 4-6 | Present but inconsistent, weakly documented, or fragile |
| 7-8 | Clear model with tests, explicit interfaces, and operational safeguards |
| 9-10 | Mature, durable, observable, extensible, and proven under failure or scale |

> Does every failure have a clear recovery path or escalation policy?

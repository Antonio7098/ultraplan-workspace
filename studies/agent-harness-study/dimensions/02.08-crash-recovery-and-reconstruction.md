# Dimension 02.08: Crash Recovery and Reconstruction

## Purpose

Study what the system does after a crash, restart, or worker loss.

## Steps

1. Identify startup recovery routines.
2. Check incomplete run handling.
3. Inspect pending tool calls and in-flight jobs.
4. Determine whether recovery resumes, retries, marks failed, or abandons.
5. Check whether recovery is tested.
6. Document operator visibility.

## Evidence to Capture

- Recovery jobs
- Startup hooks
- Run statuses
- Pending state records
- Requeue logic
- Dead-letter handling

## Questions

1. What happens to in-progress work after restart?
2. Are orphaned runs detected?
3. Is recovery automatic?
4. Is recovery idempotent?
5. Are users/operators notified?

## Rating

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Absent, implicit, ad-hoc, or unsafe |
| 4-6 | Present but inconsistent, weakly documented, or fragile |
| 7-8 | Clear model with tests, explicit interfaces, and operational safeguards |
| 9-10 | Mature, durable, observable, extensible, and proven under failure or scale |

> If power dies mid-tool-call, can the system recover cleanly?

# Dimension 23.02: Persistence vs Escalation Philosophy

## Purpose

Study whether the agent keeps trying, replans, asks for help, delegates, pauses, or stops.

## Steps

1. Identify persistence strategies: retry, replan, ask for help, delegate, pause, stop.
2. Check whether persistence is configurable.
3. Inspect escalation paths.
4. Check whether persistence decisions are logged.
5. Check whether the system can be configured for different autonomy levels.

## Evidence to Capture

- Persistence configs
- Replanning triggers
- Escalation paths
- Persistence decision logs
- Autonomy level configs

## Questions

1. Does the agent persist or escalate on failure?
2. Is persistence configurable?
3. Are escalation paths clear?
4. Are persistence decisions observable?

## Rating

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Absent, implicit, ad-hoc, or unsafe |
| 4-6 | Present but inconsistent, weakly documented, or fragile |
| 7-8 | Clear model with tests, explicit interfaces, and operational safeguards |
| 9-10 | Mature, durable, observable, extensible, and proven under failure or scale |

> Can the agent be configured to be more or less persistent depending on the task?
